Example prices above are for the Yorkshire area (Feb. 2003) using a standard credit meter. We assume level annual use, which is not recommended to calculate energy consumption accurately but is considered a valid way to compare one suppliers prices against another.

For a Yorkshire local supplier customer with an estimated annual spend of £300, paying quarterly by cheque. If using a yearly spend estimate we need to calculate how much electricity this would purchase.

  • Subtracting the Annual Standing Charge from the £300 estimate results in £258.22 remaining for electricity
  • £258.22 / 0.0633 (£ per kWh) = 4079 kWh (rounded)
  • Over the next year, assuming current prices, the customer will use
    4079 kWh of electricity.
To compare with Competitive Supplier, how much would the same amount of electricity cost?
  • 4079 kWh * 0.0562 (£ per kWh) = £229
  • Adding the annual standing charge £229.22 + £59.13 = £288.35
  • £300 Yorkshire spend less £288.35 difference = £11.65
  • Estimated savings = £ 12
Now with the extra benefit of lower direct debit prices.
  • 4079 kWh * 0.0489 (£ per kWh) = £199.46
  • Adding the annual standing charge £199.46 + £51.12 = £250.58
  • £300 Yorkshire spend less £250.58 difference = £49.42
  • Estimated savings = £ 49
If the customer was already paying Yorkshire by direct debit, they would save around £40 by switching to the Competitive Supplier.
All direct debit discounts are included in calculations.
